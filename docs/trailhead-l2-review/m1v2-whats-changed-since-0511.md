# Module 1 v2 — What Changed Since the May 11 Copy

A quick orientation note for reviewers. The current Module 1 v2 draft has moved on from the copy that was circulating for comment. If you reviewed the earlier version, here's what's different and why — so the jump isn't a surprise.

## Why there are two versions

The May 11 copy was pulled into our working repo and then went through two editing passes that weren't synced back to that copy:

1. **Validation pass** — every product claim was checked against the current Salesforce Help portal, and a per-claim citation log was produced.
2. **Evergreen-language pass** — release-version notations (260, 262, Spring '26, Summer '26) were removed so the content stays accurate across releases.

The version you're looking at now folds both passes in, plus the three editorial comments from the latest review (see below).

## Editorial comments — already applied

All three comments from the most recent review are incorporated:

- The redundant "— both under the Agentforce for Revenue Management agent suite" tail clause was trimmed from the Service bullet.
- "beyond your four walls" was replaced with plain language ("beyond your internal teams to your partners and customers").
- The "Change Agent" metaphor was removed from the Order definition — it now plainly states the Order records what changed in the customer relationship.

## Notable changes from the May 11 copy

These are wording and structure changes, not new claims:

**Unit 1**
- Learning objectives are more specific (audience named; the three business problems spelled out).
- The unit now opens with a "dominoes" framing and the "final, critical domino / connective tissue" metaphor.
- The "What's in a Name?" callout moved to after the opening paragraphs.
- Section retitled "Storytelling with a Unified Customer Record."
- The Service and Billing Inquiries subagents are now described in one combined bullet.
- The three business problems are presented as bold-label bullets rather than a table.

**Unit 2**
- Milestone plan creation is written as flowing prose rather than a bulleted list.
- Key Takeaways now use bold labels (The Golden Path, Automation Over Manual Work, Four Charge Types, Flexibility is King).

**Unit 3**
- Intro wording adjusted; no structural change.

## What did not change

No product claims were added or removed in these passes — the validation pass confirmed the existing claims, and the editorial pass was wording only. The learning objectives, unit structure, quizzes, and overall narrative arc are the same.
